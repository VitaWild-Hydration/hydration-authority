I drank four cups of coffee yesterday before noon. By 2pm I was useless. This is not a new pattern.

For most of my adult professional life, I've been on a steady three-to-five-cup-a-day routine, and for most of those years I assumed the 2pm crash was just the universal experience of office work. Coffee gets me up. Coffee keeps me going. More coffee for the slow stretch. By 5pm I'd be drained, twitchy, and oddly thirsty in a way that water wouldn't fix.

Then one of the analysts on my team — who I'd been very judgey of for ordering electrolyte drinks at the office — sent me a paper on caffeine, diuresis, and the compounding mineral loss that comes with high coffee intake. I read it. I was wrong about almost everything.

Here's the thing about your fourth coffee: it isn't waking you up. It's deepening the exact deficit that's making you tired in the first place.

Most people assume the 2pm crash is about not enough caffeine. The advice you've heard a hundred times: "switch to nitro" / "try cold brew" / "take a coffee nap" / "cut your last cup at 2pm." All fine. None addresses the actual mechanism.

But the data tells a different story. Caffeine is a mild diuretic. Each cup nudges your kidneys to release a little more water than they otherwise would. *Each cup also pulls out a small amount of sodium, potassium, and magnesium with it.* Over four cups, that's a meaningful mineral loss — and minerals are the substrate for the energy production that caffeine is *trying* to amplify. By cup four, the caffeine has nothing to amplify. The "crash" is the deficit catching up.

Here's what's actually happening:

1. You wake up moderately dehydrated. Coffee #1 wakes you up via caffeine but also accelerates fluid and mineral loss.

2. Coffee #2 around 10am compounds the loss. You're still feeling the boost.

3. Coffee #3 at lunch starts to feel less effective. The deficit is now meaningful.

4. By coffee #4 around 2pm, you're functionally mineral-depleted. The caffeine is still arriving, but your cells don't have the inputs to convert it into usable energy. The result feels like exhaustion *with* a stimulant on board — that twitchy, brain-fog, can't-focus feeling.

5. Cup #5 — if you have it — produces the same effect, plus heart palpitations and the inability to sleep that night.

Translation: the 4th coffee doesn't stop working because you've built up tolerance. It stops working because your body has run out of the minerals required to *use* the caffeine. Adding more caffeine to a mineral-depleted system is like flooring the gas pedal in a car that's out of oil.

So when you reach for *another cup* or *a stronger pull*, you're chasing the wrong lever. The fix isn't more caffeine. It's restoring the sodium, potassium, magnesium, and B-vitamins that your coffee burned through, which then allows the caffeine you've already consumed to actually function.

What you need is a real mineral dose somewhere between cup #1 and cup #3 — ideally before the crash, not after. Plain water helps a little. Plain water with electrolytes is dramatically more effective. The B-vitamin piece is what makes it actually feel like energy rather than just hydration.
